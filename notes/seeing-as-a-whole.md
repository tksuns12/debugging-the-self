# Seeing as a Whole

There was a teaching I heard from a Vipassana person.

Someone asked what “from the source” means.

He answered something like:

> Seeing as a whole is seeing from the source.

At first, this sounded like a circular explanation.

What does “from the source” mean?
Seeing as a whole.

What does “seeing as a whole” mean?
Seeing from the source.

It felt like language chasing its own tail.

But after the realization that everything is just happening, I understood it differently.

Maybe the reason it sounds circular is that the source is not something language can point to as an object.

If I can describe it as a thing, then it has already become another happening inside experience.

The source is not one more object inside the field.
It is not an inner place.
It is not a special spiritual substance.
It is not a hidden observer behind experience.

“Seeing as a whole” means seeing that even “you” are included.

What happens outside is happening.
What happens inside is happening.
The thought about the outside is happening.
The feeling about the inside is happening.
The sense of being the one who experiences inside is also happening.

So the boundary between inside and outside becomes less solid.

There is no separate inner world owned by “me” standing apart from an outer world called “reality.”

There is just one whole field of happenings.

The car on the road, the sensation in the chest, the painful wound in the heart, the thought trying to understand, and someone pooping on the other side of the Earth are all appearing in the same indivisible reality.

They are different in content.
They are different in meaning.
They are different in emotional weight.

But structurally, they are not separated into “inside me” and “outside me” in the way I had assumed.

They are all happenings.

So maybe “seeing from the source” is not seeing from a special location.

It is seeing without standing outside the whole.

It is seeing that the seer, the seen, the seeing, the interpretation, the emotion, and the question itself are all included.

That is seeing as a whole.

And because the whole cannot be turned into one object inside language, the explanation can sound circular.

But maybe it is not circular because it is careless.

Maybe it is circular because language can only move inside the thing it is trying to describe.
