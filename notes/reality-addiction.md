# Reality Addiction

A game addict treats things inside a game as if they are as precious as things in ordinary life.

A rank, an item, a virtual house, a score, a character skin, a win streak — all of these can become emotionally loaded.

From inside the game, they feel serious.
They feel worth protecting.
They feel worth fighting for.
They feel like they can define who the player is.

But from outside the game, the structure is easier to see.

It is still only a game.

The strange thing is that ordinary reality may work in a similar way.

This does not mean life is fake in a careless sense.
Pain still hurts.
People still matter.
Responsibilities still exist.
The body still needs food, sleep, warmth, and care.

But the mind can become addicted to reality in the same way it becomes addicted to a game.

It treats every gain and loss as ultimate.
It treats status as self.
It treats possessions as identity.
It treats praise, shame, success, failure, money, reputation, and even spiritual understanding as precious loot inside the game.

Then the character becomes too serious.

The player forgets that the character is happening in the game.
The ego forgets that the ego is happening in reality.
The process forgets that it is only a local process in a much larger system.

In that sense, ordinary attachment is a kind of reality addiction.

Not because reality should be hated.
Not because nothing matters.
Not because the body and the world should be neglected.

But because the extra seriousness is optional.

A game can be played sincerely without being worshiped.
A story can be lived fully without being mistaken for the final truth.
A character can be cared for without being treated as the absolute owner of the whole screen.

Maybe this is the lighter way:

Play the game.
Take care of the character.
Do the necessary quests.
Help other characters when possible.
Enjoy beauty when it appears.
Respond to pain when it appears.

But do not be too into it.

It is not worth giving the whole heart to the inventory of the game.
It is not worth turning every temporary scene into an ultimate identity.
It is not worth forgetting the screen because the movie became intense.

Reality continues.
The character continues.
The quests continue.

But the grip can loosen.

The world can be taken seriously enough to function,
and lightly enough not to be trapped by it.
