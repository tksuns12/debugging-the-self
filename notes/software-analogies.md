# Software Analogies

These are possible software engineering analogies for the realization.

## Events

Every experience can be seen as an event.

External event:

- sound
- light
- movement
- body sensation

Internal event:

- thought
- emotion
- memory
- interpretation
- the sense of being an observer

The surprising part is that the observer is not outside the event stream.
The observer is also part of the event stream.

## Logs

Thoughts are like logs.

They appear.
They report something.
They may be useful or noisy.
But a log line does not need to be treated as the owner of the system.

A thought saying “I am hurt” is also a happening.
A thought saying “I understand” is also a happening.
A thought saying “this is important” is also a happening.

## Root process

Before the realization, it felt like there was a central root process called “me.”

After the realization, even that “me” seemed to be another process arising inside the system.

Not the controller.
Not outside the system.
Not separate.

Just another happening.

## Ownership layer

Suffering may include an extra ownership layer:

- pain happens
- thought labels it as “my pain”
- identity forms around it
- resistance appears
- the pain becomes heavier

The realization did not delete pain.
It weakened the automatic ownership layer around pain.
